Omar had lived his whole life as a refugee. He had been born in a refugee camp. His parents had fled their country before he was born. Omar had never known stability. He had lived in camps and temporary housing his entire life. He went to underfunded schools and grew up in poverty. Everyone around Omar seemed to accept their circumstances as permanent. But Omar had other ideas. He studied hard and got excellent grades. He was accepted to a university with a scholarship. His parents cried tears of joy. Omar was the first person in his family to attend university. In college, Omar struggled. He felt like he didn't belong. The other students had grown up with security and resources. Omar worked while he studied. He had to work much harder than his peers just to keep up. But Omar was determined. He graduated. He got a job. He saved money. He helped his family move into permanent housing. He helped his younger siblings attend college. Omar's life story transformed from one of desperation to one of achievement. He realized that his background, as difficult as it was, had given him something valuable. It had given him resilience and determination. Omar became an advocate for refugees. He helped others from refugee backgrounds access education and opportunity. He learned that our circumstances of birth don't determine our destiny. Our choices do.
