Grace had a complicated relationship with her mother. They didn't get along. They argued frequently. Grace felt like her mother never understood her or supported her choices. When Grace was thirty, her mother was diagnosed with terminal cancer. Grace spent her mother's final months caring for her. During those months, they talked more honestly than they ever had before. Grace's mother apologized for the ways she had hurt her. She explained that she had done her best, even though it wasn't perfect. Grace found forgiveness and compassion for her mother. When her mother died, Grace was heartbroken, but she was also at peace. She had been able to repair their relationship before it was too late. Grace learned that our time with people is limited. We don't know when it will end. It's important to work toward forgiveness and understanding while we still can. Grace became an advocate for family reconciliation. She helped others repair broken relationships while there was still time.
