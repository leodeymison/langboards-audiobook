Priya had suffered sexual assault. The trauma had affected her deeply. She had PTSD. She struggled with trust and safety. She spent years in therapy working through her trauma. She fought to reclaim her life. Years later, Priya was healed enough to talk about her experience publicly. She started speaking at schools and universities about sexual assault awareness and prevention. She helped victims understand that what happened to them was not their fault. She advocated for better support systems for survivors. Priya's advocacy helped change policies. It helped create safer environments for vulnerable people. It helped countless survivors feel less alone. Priya transformed her deepest pain into her life's work. She learned that we can take something that was meant to destroy us and transform it into something that saves others.
