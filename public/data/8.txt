Jennifer grew up in poverty. Her parents worked multiple jobs, but there was rarely enough food on the table. Jennifer wore clothes that didn't fit properly, and other children made fun of her. She felt ashamed of her circumstances. But Jennifer's mother was a woman of incredible strength. Every night, despite their poverty, she read books to Jennifer. She filled Jennifer's mind with stories and possibilities. Her mother always said, "Our circumstances don't define who we can become." Jennifer held onto those words. She worked hard in school. She got a scholarship to college. She was the first person in her family to attend university. In college, Jennifer studied hard, but she also struggled with the shame of her background. She was ashamed that her family didn't have money. One day, a professor asked her about her background. Jennifer finally shared her story. The professor told her that her journey was her superpower. She had overcome real obstacles. This made her stronger and more resilient than many of her privileged classmates. Jennifer graduated and became an advocate for underprivileged children. She built her career on helping kids like her younger self. She never forgot where she came from, and she always used her success to lift others up.
