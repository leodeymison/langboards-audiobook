David had been bullied his entire childhood. He was the smallest kid in his class, and bullies targeted him mercilessly. Every school day was a nightmare. David wanted to disappear. He didn't believe he had any value. When he grew older, David realized that his childhood pain had given him something unexpected: empathy and compassion. He understood what it felt like to be invisible and unwanted. As an adult, David became a counselor for bullied children. He spent his career helping young people who felt the way he had felt. He taught them that their pain had meaning. He showed them that they could transform their suffering into strength. David's work saved lives. Many young people who came to see him were suicidal. Through his care and guidance, they found reasons to keep living. David transformed his greatest pain into his greatest purpose. He learned that what we suffer often becomes what we are meant to heal in others.
